Grundmodelle (Modelle 1–4)

Prof. Dr. Wolfram Kircher, Hochschule Anhalt und Prof. Dr. Andreas Thon, Hochschule Geisenheim – neu überarbeitet 2025

  • The mineralizing effect of slow-percolated filters demands a downstream Hydrobotanical System to discharge phosphorus.
  • A great deal of space is necessary: FLL (2017) recommends at least 8 × 4 m for the swimming area or a diameter of at least 5 m.
  • Purification factors, P-content and maintenance demands are compa- rable to model 1 pools, as is the possibility of C-limitation.
  • If the fill-up water with low hardness such as rainwater is available, C-limitation may be easier to achieve than P-limitation.
  • We rec- ommend building at least 30% of the swimming zone at least 2.5 m deep, so that accumulated mud is not stirred up by swimmers.
  • It inevitably uses a naturalistic design and a lower visual depth must be accepted, but maintenance demands can be kept low.
  • Part-flooded (emersed) TWLs fed top-down by irrigating water onto the surface can run intermittently, for example in 30-minute cycles.
  • Nevertheless it is desirable to keep the phosphorus content below 0.03mg Ptot/l on aver- age (≙ about 0.1 mg phosphates/l).
  • Assessment and recommendations The swimming pond is the most cost-effective solution for nature-loving plant enthusiasts.
  • To minimize the nutrient content, the mud has to be vacuumed up at least once a year and plants trimmed in early autumn.
