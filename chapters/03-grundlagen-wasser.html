Grundlagen Wasser (Biologie, Chemie, Hygiene)

Prof. Dr. Wolfram Kircher, Hochschule Anhalt und Prof. Dr. Andreas Thon, Hochschule Geisenheim – neu überarbeitet 2025

  • After a sustainable EPS has developed, growth proceeds and more and more microbes settle, increasing the thickness of the biofilm.
  • Microlayers of biofilm can also occur along the interface between water and air (Costerton et al. 1995) and form dull residue films.
  • Natural pools on the other hand are characterized by very low nutrient levels and if not Bell animalcules are a component of the biofilm.
  • BIOFILM: THE PURIFICATION FACTORY The “overall underwater coat” The surfaces of all underwater structures provide a habitat for microor- ganisms to grow.
  • A high oxygen supply can slow but not stop this process. • In the “final phase of ageing”, parts of the biofilm die off and are removed from the matrix.
  • The stronger the inflow towards surfaces, the more intensive the development of biofilm layers will be.
  • Beneficial biofilm in water filtration The great purification power of the biofilm is the central component of special filter beds in natural swimming pools.
  • The entire surface of the gravel aggregates provides an enormous substratum for biofilm development.
  • Skimmers for cleaning the surface should either produce a low current or operate intermittently. • Detached flakes from the ageing biofilm will float on the surface.
  • Dissolved nutrients, germs and pollutants provide nourishment for biofilm microorganisms.
